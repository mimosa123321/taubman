China’s numbers dwarf the US

While Paypal has been around much longer and Apple Pay garners more headlines, the real players in mobile payments are in China.

6 trillion RMB ( US$ 98 billion) – Gross merchandise volume (GMV) of mobile payments in China in 2014 – that’s FIVE times the total in 2013, according to statistics from iResearch. The US market totaled US$ 52 billion in 2014

62% – Total share of online purchases in China predicted to be conducted via mobile by 2018 - total value to exceed 18 trillion RMB

Drivers for China’s surge in mobile payments:

  • China is the largest mobile market in the world – 1.3 trillion phones in use in 2014
  • Low credit card adoption therefore mobile and online payments in demand

Alipay vs Tenpay vs Paypal

China’s innovation potential:

Given the size and growth of user base, plus the broader eco-system of the Chinese players, the potential for innovation is much greater in China.

Alibaba has remained focused on core e-commerce transactions while Alipay and Tenpay is more embedded in other parts of user’s lifestyles with more features and services available on its platforms.
